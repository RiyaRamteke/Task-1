Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj

Born: 19 February 1630, Shivneri Fort, Kusur

Shivaji Maharaj, also known as Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, was a legendary Indian warrior king and the founder of the Maratha Empire in western India in the 17th century. He was born in 1630 in the Shivneri Fort in Maharashtra, India. Shivaji Maharaj is revered for his military acumen, administrative skills, and his efforts to establish a sovereign Hindu state, free from foreign domination. Shivaji Maharaj's reign is marked by numerous military campaigns, guerrilla tactics, and strategic alliances. He utilized the geography of the region to his advantage and pioneered the concept of guerrilla warfare known as "Ganimi Kava" (the art of warfare). His campaigns against the Mughal Empire, Adil Shahi Sultanate, and other regional powers played a significant role in expanding Maratha influence. Shivaji Maharaj's legacy remains deeply ingrained in Indian history and culture. He is celebrated as a symbol of resistance against tyranny and foreign rule. His principles of leadership, valor, and governance continue to inspire generations of Indians.
